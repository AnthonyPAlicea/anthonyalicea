## Introducing Cascade Methodology

It includes concepts such as:

* **Minimum Solved Problems (MSP)** ‚Äî a problem-focused replacement for MVP.

## The 7 Core Principles of Cascade Methodology

1. Everyone has a seat at the table in writing specs.
2. Understand the underlying user and business problems the software should solve.
3. Build the least amount of software possible to solve those problems.
4. Prototyping and user research are first-class citizens.
5. AI-generated code is not to be trusted.
6. Code quality checks scale with the **entropy tolerance** of the business process.
7. Scope and timelines depend on entropy tolerance and problem understanding.

Cascades give LLMs the context they need while protecting against the risks of probabilistic code generation. It‚Äôs about embracing AI‚Äôs speed while staying grounded in solving real problems with quality software.

## How Do Cascades Differ from Agile, Scrum, or Lean?

### Ceremonies

* **Agile/Scrum:** Sprint planning, standups, grooming, retrospectives ‚Äî often time-wasting and burnout-inducing.
* **Cascade:** Continuous spec writing and user research with everyone contributing. Specs become strong LLM context.

### Estimates

* **Agile/Scrum:** Story points and forecasts ‚Äî often guesswork and morale killers when missed.
* **Cascade:** Effort buckets based on entropy tolerance ‚Äî the lower the tolerance, the more checks required.

### Velocity

* **Agile/Scrum:** Story points completed per sprint.
* **Cascade:** Problems solved across a time period.

### Measure of Progress

* **Agile:** ‚ÄúWorking software is the primary measure of progress.‚Äù
* **Cascade:** Solved problems are the measure of progress.

### LLM Code Generation Risk Mitigation

* **Agile/Scrum:** None ‚Äî not designed for AI-assisted development.
* **Cascade:** Core concept ‚Äî scalable verification gates for AI-generated code.

### MVP vs. MSP

* **Agile/Lean:** MVP (Minimum Viable Product) ‚Äî smallest *product* possible, often based on someone‚Äôs favorite feature.
* **Cascade:** MSP (Minimum Solved Problem) ‚Äî smallest *meaningful problem* solved, which may not require new software at all.

Sometimes solving the problem doesn‚Äôt require more software ‚Äî or requires far less than requested.
**Your software development practices should excite, encourage, and enable, not exhaust.**
